Anthony, this is the picture we built from your walkthrough — simplified to two boxes: your Salesforce org on the left in light blue, and everything external on the right in grey. Leads come from web forms and live chat. Anything coming in from a partner org flows through Salesforce-to-Salesforce sharing, both ways. Standard CRM works — and you mentioned the record types across the four divisions have all been merged into one process, which is huge. The Print Estimator is the Apex class that runs cost-plus on a bell curve and writes a recommended price — reps adjust freely from there, no system-enforced approvals. On Closed-Won, the Apex trigger on Opportunity creates Job__c; QuoteLineItems are copied into Job_Item__c, which is where all your custom attributes live (~450 fields). From there, Job_Item__c fans out to Fulfillment__c for shipments — one Job_Item can ship to many states — and Invoice_Line_Item__c is the junction that ties Job_Items and Fulfillments to a single Invoice__c. Critically, every invoice must start in Salesforce before passing to Business Central; that's the lever that gets the other divisions to enter data. Foam, BoxPop, and Wood still rely on standalone tools — the foam team has 3D design software, the woodworking team has that 30-year-old cabinet database. Art lives in Adobe Illustrator. Avalara handles tax, BC handles finance and supply chain, Authorize.net for cards (and a lot of checks/ACH still). If any of this is off, please stop me — the rest of the conversation depends on getting this right.
